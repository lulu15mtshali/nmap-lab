# Soicial Engineering Reasearh Report 

## Types of Attacks 
-**Phising**: Sending fake emails disguised as trustworthy entities to seal credentials or deploy malware.
-**Pretexting**: Creating a fake scenario (a pretext) to manipulate a person into releasing information or performing an action.
-**Bating**: Promising an item or good (like a free movie download or a malware-loaded USB stick left in a parking lot) to entice a person into a trap.

## Case Studies 
-**Phishing (Sony Pictures, 2014)**: Attackers use targeted phising emails to steal executive credntials, leading to a masive data breach and wiper malware deployment.
-**Pretexting (Hewllett-Packed, 2006)**: Private investigators used pretexting to impersonate board members and reporters, obtaining private phone records illegally.

## Prevention Recommendations
1. **Security Awareness Training**: Train employees regularly to spot fake emails addresses and suspicious urgent requests.
2. **Multi-factor Authentiation**: Enforce MFA across all systems so stolen credentials alone aren't enough to compromise accounts.
3. **Strict Verfiation Policies**: Establish firm rules to verify any identity over the phone or email before sharing sensitive data.
